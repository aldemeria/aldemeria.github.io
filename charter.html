Charter of the Conclave of Aldemeria

I. Establishment of the Conclave of Aldemeria

The realms signatory to this Charter, in the spirit of establishing and maintaining greater relations between our peoples and opportunities for collaboration in areas of diplomacy, trade, defense, and cultural exchange, do affirm their membership of the Conclave of Aldemeria.

This international forum is created with the purpose of fostering a more profitable dialogue between the realms of Aldemeria, and allowing representatives of all member realms to enact decisions for the betterment of all peoples.

Members of the Conclave hereby agree to abide by an international legal structure such as will provide for the safeguarding and advancement of a free and just society, and will enable representatives of all realms to be recognized by the Conclave and create statutes binding across all member realms.

II. Membership of the Conclave

a. Individuals recognized as titled by the Conclave of Aldemeria shall be considered members of the Conclave, absolutely equal under the law and among fellow members.
b. Membership of the Conclave of Aldemeria may be conferred upon a non-member by way of a vote in support of conferral of membership of at least five votes from noble members of the Conclave, with each noble casting a single vote regardless of rank, in conjunction with a formal vote of approval from the sitting Minister of Applications. Individuals upon whom membership has been conferred shall be granted the title of Commoner unless explicitly ruled otherwise.
c. Upon acceptance as a Commoner of the Conclave of Aldemeria, the Commoner is welcomed to Endelvalyia until the completion of the Liege offer process. Offers for settlement may be made by prospective Lieges for 24 hours after their acceptance to the Conclave of Aldemeria. After that time, no more offers may be made.
d. If a noble member of the Conclave wishes to relinquish himself of a vassal commoner, he may do so by way of a formal posting to the Hall of Nations. The commoner is thus left landless and has 5 days to appeal or entertain other liege offers within the posting. If no lieges present offers or the commoner accepts none within the period, the commoner is no longer considered a member of the Conclave of Aldemeria. They may re-apply if they are not able to find a liege within 5 days.
e. If an application receives 5 no votes from noble members of the Conclave of Aldemeria, they may be banned from all applicable areas.
f. If a noble member of the Conclave of Aldemeria wishes to abdicate his/her realm, they can do so by posting a notice of realm abdication in the Hall of Nations. After doing so, the noble member has two options. They may found a new realm, following all normal realm creation laws, OR they may become a vassal in another nation if that nation is willing to take them in.The abdicated noble must join a realm who is a higher rank then they are, however they may voluntarily lower their rank to any rank that is below their current rank.

III. Definitions

a. Definition of a County

A county is the most basic political division in the Conclave of Aldemeria. A county may be composed of a variety of settlements that can differ in size, composition, and number. The first county of a realm is expected to have at least one settlement containing a minimum of 25 buildings, as well as including 2 additional settlements each containing a minimum of 5 buildings. Particularly large structures may be used in place of a hamlet. Additional counties in a realm may differ in composition. These additional political divisions are required to contain a minimum of 35 buildings to meet the definition of a county. Together, these settlements must include forms of residential presence, several industries, economy, cultural development, secure infrastructure and connections to other locations as appropriate.
The number of structures required may be more than 35, if any of the following are true:

  • The proposed county does not have at least three settlements.
  • The proposed county is not the first county of a realm, and contains no settlements of at least 25 buildings.
  • The average building size within the county is considered small.

b. Definition of Titles

For the purposes of administration and governance, the Conclave of Aldemeria shall formally recognize the following international titles as applying to members of the Conclave, in order of greatest to least authority:
Emperor / Empress
King / Queen
Prince / Princess
Duke / Duchess
Count / Countess
Lord / Lady
Esquire
Commoner

Holders of each and every title shall be equal under the law and among their peers as members of Aldemeria; members holding the rank of Lord or Lady upwards shall be additionally recognized as noble and shall hold additional abilities including the right to vote, claim land, serve on a Conclave jury, and run for elected office.

I. While these titles alone may be recognized in Conclave law, this Conclave shall make no attempt to standardize rank and title structures internationally, and shall expressly encourage the cultivation of local naming conventions.

II. In cases where one citizen resides in a realm controlled by a higher ranking individual, they are considered to be a vassal, and the higher ranking member is their liege. The highest power in each realm is considered to be the liege of every other member of their realm, with everyone else being their vassals, but other liege vassal relationships may exist within realms as well. I.e. a Prince could have a Duke, who has a Count, who may be considered the vassal of both the Prince and Duke, or just the Prince, subject to the discretion of the highest authority of the realm.

III. All non-noble members of the Conclave (Commoner and Esquire) must reside in a realm under the jurisdiction of a liege that is noble (Lord or Higher). Upon reaching the rank of Lord, they may elect to claim land under the land claim laws and start their own realm, or remain within the same realm if they wish. Vassals also reserve the right to change lieges as long as the new liege is willing to accept them as a vassal.

IV. Title Ascension

a. A member of the Conclave may be ascended in title under either of two circumstances; either the member resides under the jurisdiction of a noble liege, in which case the ascension shall take place at the full discretion of the liege, or the member resides independently, under the jurisdiction of the Conclave Assembly, in which case the ascension shall be at the full discretion of the Conclave Assembly and shall be enacted with a greater than 50% vote in support of a motion for ascension. Members of the realm going for ascension who possess votes within the conclave shall not be counted for the ascension in any way. Ascension requests are open for two weeks for applications towards Duke and lower. Ascension requests are open for four weeks for applications towards Prince and above. After this time, applications are considered to have failed. Applicants may re-apply at any time. Within the context of Ascension Applications, abstained votes count as no votes.
b. If a vassal within a realm is promoted to the rank of Lord/Lady or higher, a formal notice must be provided to the Conclave by the Liege, at this time, if any noble member of the conclave deems the promotion premature, they have the right to call for a vote against the ascension, this vote must reach a total of greater than 50% of the active votes in the council, following all standard voting procedures and activity laws.
c. The following requirements for ascension of independent realms are not to be followed strictly to the letter. Rather, they are meant to be guidelines of what the Conclave deems proper for a member of that rank. Ultimately, it is up to the discretion of the Conclave as to whether the member is deserving of such a rank, given that the member in question can reasonably justify deficiencies in any of the following statements. The same is expected of lieges promoting a vassal; while keeping to the international standard is expressly encouraged, the liege has express power over internal ascensions.
d. The following requirements are cumulative, with each higher step necessarily including all beneath.
e. For the Commoner seeking ascension to the title of Esquire, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of one or more works beneficial to the function of the realm in which they reside.
  • Regular and consistent activity and engagement within the Conclave consistent with an individual who would remain as such.
  • The basic compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their immediate person, including but not limited to personal history, genealogy, and future ambitions.
f. For the Esquire seeking ascension to the title of Lord/Lady, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of a developed hold. This hold shall consist of at least 20 buildings, being of sufficient size and diversity as to provide for such activity as forms of residential presence, several industries, economy, cultural development, secure infrastructure and connections to other locations as appropriate. The hold can be composed of either a single settlement or multiple settlements.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who would remain a positive contributor to the development of the community. This includes but is not limited to: Forum responses, legal contributions, discord activity, participating in lore events, etc.
  • The detailed compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their character and their holding for the purpose of building the foundation for future lore and world-building. This includes, however is not limited to the background and development of their character, and the economic, political, and development of their holding. This can come in the form of posts on the forums and/or development of pages on the wiki.
  • Should an Esquire's liege become inactive for more than two months, they may petition the Council of Ministers to apply for a rank ascension to Lord. This petition must be posted in the 'Hall of Nations' section of the Conclave Governance sub-forum. If by a greater than 50% vote the Council of Ministers votes in favor of the petition, the Esquire may post a request for rank ascension to the same sub-forum. If the Esquire applying for ascension has but a Lord for a liege, the Esquire's vote for rank ascension must also be accompanied by a proposal to found a new realm.
g. For the Lord/Lady seeking ascension to the title of Count/Countess, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of at least one developed County, where a County shall be defined as a developed, interconnected region including at least one larger settlement (consisting of a more detailed settlement than what is required for a Lord, of at least 25 buildings), and a variety of other settlements (together these settlements must combine to form the standard number of buildings required for a County) that together include forms of residential presence, several industries, economy, cultural development, secure infrastructure and connections to other locations as appropriate.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who is increasing their role as a positive contributor to the development of the community. This includes but is not limited to: Forum responses, legal contributions, discord activity, participating in lore events, etc. Lords seeking to ascend to Count are expected to vote on Conclave matters, discuss legal topics, host and/or participate in lore events, contribute to co-builds and/or build in other realms, and participate actively in other server functions.
  • The detailed compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their character and their holding for the purpose of building the foundation for future lore and world-building, including future goals. This includes, however is not limited to: the background and development of their character; the development of their language, religion, species, race, and other culture; the economic, political, and development of their county. This can come in the form of posts on the forums and/or development of pages on the wiki.
h. For the Count/Countess seeking ascension to the title of Duke/Duchess, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of at least one developed Duchy, where a Duchy shall be defined as a developed, interconnected region including at least three counties, with one containing a larger settlement. This larger settlement must consist of at least 40 buildings, and include several buildings that indicate its more important functions as the capital of a Duchy. Should vassals assist in the construction of this duchy, the capital county of the Duchy must be primarily constructed by the Count/Countess seeking ascension to the title of Duke/Duchess.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who is increasing their role as a positive contributor to the development of the community. This includes but is not limited to: Forum responses, legal contributions, discord activity, participating in lore events, etc. Counts seeking to ascend to Duke are expected to vote on Conclave matters, discuss legal topics, serve as Deputy Ministers and/or Ministers of the Conclave, host lore events, lead co-builds and/or builds in other realms - including international projects, and participate actively in other server functions.
  • The detailed compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their character and their holding for the purpose of building the foundation for future lore and world-building, including future goals. This includes, however is not limited to: the background and development of their character; the development of their language, religion, species, race, and other culture; the economic, political, and development of their duchy. For the Count/Countess seeking ascension to the title of Duke/Duchess, this must come in the form of posts on the forums and development of pages on the wiki. The Count/Countess seeking ascension to the title of Duke/Duchess is expected to have a very well developed wiki and forum presence.
i. For the Duke/Duchess seeking ascension to the title of Prince/Princess, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of at least one detailed and developed Principality, where a Principality shall be defined as a developed, interconnected region including at least three duchies, with one containing a larger settlement. This larger settlement must consisting of at least 100 detailed buildings, and include several buildings that indicate its more important functions as the capital of a Principality. Should vassals assist in the construction of this Principality, the capital Duchy of the Principality must be primarily constructed by the Duke/Duchess seeking ascension to the title of Prince/Princess.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who is increasing their role as a positive contributor to the development of the community. This includes but is not limited to: Forum responses, legal contributions, discord activity, participating in lore events, etc. Dukes seeking to ascend to Prince are expected to vote on Conclave matters, discuss legal topics, serve as Deputy Ministers and/or Ministers of the Conclave, host lore events, lead co-builds and/or builds in other realms - including international projects, and participate actively in other server functions.
  • The detailed compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their character and their holding for the purpose of building the foundation for future lore and world-building, including future goals. This includes, however is not limited to: the background and development of their character; the development of their language, religion, species, race, and other culture; the economic, political, and development of their county. For the Duke/Duchess seeking ascension to the title of Prince/Princess, this must come in the form of posts on the forums and development of pages on the wiki. The Duke/Duchess seeking ascension to the title of Prince/Princess is expected to have a very well developed wiki and forum presence that includes collaboration with other realms over a long length of time on the server.
j. For the Prince / Princess seeking ascension to the title of King / Queen, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of at least one kingdom, where a kingdom shall be defined as a developed, interconnected region including at least two principalities, with one containing one settlement (A settlement of unprecedented size connected to many settlements, etc.; many landmarks; a center of industries and business), being of sufficient size and diversity as to provide for such activity as forms of residential presence, industry, economy, cultural development, secure infrastructure and connections to other locations as appropriate, etc.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who would remain a positive contributor to the development of the community.
  • The complete compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their immediate person as well as all aspects of their realm, including but not limited to history of the realm, significance of the realm within the world at large (for example in regards to economic and industrial activity), census data, culture and philosophy, future goals, etc.
k. For the King / Queen seeking ascension to the title of Emperor / Empress, the authority charged with effecting ascension shall require of the individual:
  • The physical construction of at least one kingdom, where a kingdom shall be defined as a developed, interconnected region including at least two principalities, with one containing one settlement (A settlement of unprecedented size connected to many settlements, etc.; many landmarks; a center of industries and business), being of sufficient size and diversity as to provide for such activity as forms of residential presence, industry, economy, cultural development, secure infrastructure and connections to other locations as appropriate, etc.
  • The physical construction of an additional number of duchies equivalent to a kingdom (6), differing from the first kingdom culturally, architecturally, and/or politically. These duchies can either form a single, separate polity, or may exist as individual polities or of any combination.
  • Regular and consistent activity and constructive participation within the Conclave consistent with an individual who would remain a positive contributor to the development of the community.
  • The complete compilation and drafting of lore and records concerning their immediate person as well as all aspects of their realm, including but not limited to history of the realm, significance of the realm within the world at large (for example in regards to economic and industrial activity), census data, culture and philosophy, future goals, etc.

V. The Conclave Assembly

a. The Conclave Assembly shall serve as the main legislative body of the Conclave, and shall be composed of all active noble (those with a title of Lord/Lady or higher) members. The Assembly shall hold a solemn duty to represent the will of the people as a whole, and is entrusted with the building of broad consensus in order to enact decisions for the betterment of the Conclave and its members. To this end, the Assembly is charged with all key legislative affairs, including the proposal of new legislative statutes and the amending of current law where the assembly judges appropriate.
b. In order to reflect the experience and knowledge of members of the Conclave, noble members shall be granted a number of votes in the Assembly in accordance with their title:
  • Emperor / Empress: 5 Votes Granted
  • King / Queen: 5 Votes Granted
  • Prince / Princess: 4 Votes Granted
  • Duke / Duchess: 3 Votes Granted
  • Count / Countess: 2 Votes Granted
  • Lord / Lady: 1 Vote Granted
c. With the formal opening of a legislative motion for voting, members of the Assembly shall have a period of two weeks in order to record a vote for or against the motion, with a vote conferring all votes granted in accordance with the members' titles. At the conclusion of the voting period, all votes shall be tallied. A motion shall be passed if greater than 50% of all votes recorded are in support; otherwise it shall have failed and must require a period of at least two weeks discussion prior to being reintroduced.
d. The above requirements for the passing of legislation in the Assembly shall hold true in all cases unless other requirements are explicitly specified.
e. Realms that receive recognition from the Conclave of Aldemeria, must affix their signature, in the style of all previous signatures, to the Charter, to receive their votes. Any realm that does not sign the charter has no votes and is ineligible for rank ascensions until such a time as they do so.

VI. The Council of Ministers

a. In order to better manage and foster progress in diverse areas of Conclave policy, history, culture, and law, the Conclave of Aldemeria shall recognize the Council of Ministers; this elected body, accountable to the people, shall guide policy in certain areas and shall serve as an assembly of knowledge and experience for the Conclave as a whole.
The Conclave shall recognize the following Ministerial positions:
  • Minister of Applications, charged with coordinating the welcoming of new members to the Conclave and the guiding of policy in regards to the process by which new members may be admitted.
  • Minister of Infrastructure, charged with the oversight and the review of the Conclave's physical infrastructure throughout all worlds and means of transportation, and the guiding of policy in regards to the development of infrastructure.
  • Minister of Lore, charged with compiling and fostering the appreciation of the history, culture, and beliefs of the peoples of the Conclave, and the guiding of policy in regards to the clarification of disputed records.
  • Minister of Justice, charged with maintaining a free and just legal system, with representing impartiality and neutrality as a representative of the Conclave in judicial matters, and with the guiding of policy in regards to the interpretation of disputed law.
b. The Conclave shall reserve the right at any time to create, alter, and remove Ministerial positions; all Ministers shall forever be equal under the law, and newly-created Ministerial positions shall function according to the same restrictions of this Charter. The Conclave Assembly shall formally create, alter, or remove a Ministerial position with a greater than 66% vote in support of the motion.
c. The Council of Ministers shall hold the solemn duty to act as a sober second thought for the decisions of the Conclave Assembly; it shall not seek to stand as a barrier to the desires of the peoples of the Conclave nor shall it attempt to concentrate authority in the hands of a few, but shall act to provide the Conclave with the benefit of the experience and knowledge of its members. To this end, the Council shall reserve the right to veto any legislative motion passed by the Assembly. A veto must receive the support of at least 75% of sitting Ministers and must be accompanied by a justification for the action; a successful veto shall return the motion to the Assembly where it may be further discussed and/or voted upon again at the discretion of the Assembly. If a motion is passed with greater than 66% of votes in support, it is recognized as carried by the broad will of the people and may not be subject to veto.
d. Members of the Council shall be held accountable to the Assembly and to the peoples of the Conclave through fair and equal elections held for each Ministerial position every four months, beginning July 1st, 2017 The commencement of the election period shall be marked by a formal announcement from the sitting members, from which point all noble members of the Conclave shall have a nomination period of one week in which to submit themselves for consideration for a Ministerial position. Immediately following this a one-week voting period shall take place, in which every noble member may cast a single, equal vote for a candidate in any or all elections. At the conclusion of the voting period, all votes shall be tallied in each election; the candidate having received a plurality of votes shall be designated the next Minister. Newly elected ministers assume their position two weeks following the beginning of the nomination period. In a case where a single candidate submits themselves to run for a position, the candidate shall have run unopposed and shall become the next Minister. In a case where no candidate submits themselves to run for a position, the Minister to have held the position at the commencement of the election shall retain their position. In the case of a tied election where two or more candidates receive an equally-greatest number of votes, the election may immediately be re-run, subject to the same restrictions of the previous, with those candidates only.
e. A Minister may leave office outside of an election under two circumstances; if the sitting Minister resigns, or if the Assembly, judging the Minister to not have respected the charter duties of their position or to otherwise be unwilling or incapable of acting as Minister, successfully carries a motion to remove the Minister from office with a greater than 66% vote in support of the motion. In the event that a Minister has left office outside of general election, a special election shall immediately be convened and shall run according to the normal electioneer procedure; the new Minister shall hold the position until the next regular election.
f. The Ministers are hereby given the power to publicly enact policies related to their current position. These policies can be changed at any time by the respective current minister. Any such policy can be put to vote to be struck down or recognized more permanently as a Conclave Law under normal voting procedure applies.
g. Any member of the Conclave is given permission to file a Lore Grievance against another member of the Conclave on the forums. The accused will have a week to defend themselves, and any member of the Conclave is free to voice their opinion on the matter. At the conclusion of the discussion period, the Minister of Lore will voice his decision as to the standing of the lore in question.
h. If the Minister of Lore feels an article of lore does not fit into the current server canon, then a Lore Grievance will be posted with the Minister as the accuser. After a week, the Minister can declare the lore non-canon except in cases of clear support from the Conclave (a majority of responses in favor of keeping the debated lore).
i. Each individual Minister shall reserve the right to designate up to two Deputy Ministers in fulfilling the duties of the Minister; responsibility for the actions of these individuals shall be assumed by the Minister. Deputy Ministers may be assigned or removed at any time by their Minister. Deputy Ministers are dismissed when a single Minister term ends, when a Minister resigns, or a Minister is impeached.

VII. Rights and Liberties

a. All members of the conclave have the following Freedoms of Action and Thought;
  • Speech, Expression, Assembly, Association, Press and Media
  • Thought, Belief, Conscience, and Opinion
  • Movement through all lands of the Conclave and its Members
b. All members of the conclave have the following Rights of Fair Treatment
  • Liberty from discrimination and hate speech based on sex, race, ethnic or national origins, creed or religion, physical or mental ability, or sexuality
  • Right to equal treatment before and under the law, and equal benefit and protection from the law regardless of sex, race, ethnic or national origins, creed or religion, physical or mental ability, or sexuality
c. All members of the conclave have the following Legal and Judicial Rights
  • Right to a trial as outlined in the Charter within a reasonable period of being arrested and/or charged with a crime
  • Liberty from cruel and unusual punishments or treatment
  • Liberty from unreasonable searches and seizures
  • Liberty from arbitrary detention or imprisonment
  • Liberty from any presumption of guilt unless proven in a court of law
  • Liberty from testifying against oneself in a trial or arbitration hearing or any coercion to give false testimony in a trial or arbitration hearing
  • Right to a new trial if new evidence emerges or if judicial procedure was not followed properly
  • Liberty from being charged with a crime not contained within this Charter or the any Law of Aldemeria at the time of the alleged violation.
  • Liberty from being charged with a violation of a contract, treaty, or agreement based on action taken when the contract, treaty, or agreement was not in effect

VIII. Conclave Criminal Code

The following actions are considered to be criminal infractions against the Conclave of Aldemeria, and as such, charges against an individual that is accused of committing one or more of these actions may be prosecuted by the Conclave Justice Minister:
a. Homicide Homicide is the intentional or accidental non-consensual death, also known as murdering, of another person outside of the bounds of any form of war or consensual PvP actions. An individual that feels as if they are threatened with their death or some other form of serious harm may legally defend themselves against non-consensual attacks, including causing the death of their assailant.
b. Theft Theft is defined as the removal from their original location of items, entities, mobs, villagers or blocks without the permission of the owner of the items, entities, mobs, villagers or blocks. This includes all items in chests, shulker boxes, item frames, armor stands; mobs, both tamed and untamed and villagers; blocks that exist in a realm, including those not placed by the player.
c. Griefing Griefing is defined as the non-consensual alteration of blocks, entities, mobs, or villagers belonging to an individual, realm, or the Conclave as a whole. This includes alteration via TNT, lava, fire, killing, removing, replacing, or building. Building without permission in other realms is also considered griefing.
d. Duping Duping is defined as exploiting the game to create or reproduce additional items.
e. Evidence The spoliation/tampering with evidence is defined as the destruction, negligent withholding, fabrication, or altering of items both physical and otherwise that can be or will be used as evidence in a judicial trial.
f. False Testimony False testimony is defined as bearing false witness during judicial procedures, which includes all activity that occurs in the Court of Arbitration, the Hall of Nations, during Conclave votes, and during Civil or Criminal Trials.
g. Community Behavior All individuals accepted to the Conclave of Aldemeria are expected to respect and behave in accordance to the the Rights and Liberties section of the Conclave Charter. Undermining the Rights and Liberties through actions, behavior, and words on discord, in game, or on the forums is not acceptable and may be prosecuted by the Conclave of Aldemeria.

IX. Judiciary

a. The Conclave of Aldemeria defines two types of Judiciary Trials; Civil and Criminal. Criminal Trials are defined as a court proceeding originating from one or more citizens of the Conclave breaking one or more international laws of the Conclave of Aldemeria. A Civil Trial is defined as a court proceeding originating from one or more citizens bringing their grievances with one or more other citizens before the Ministry of Justice, seeking a mediated solution.
b. During a Criminal trial, the Minister of Justice is the preferred person to act as Arbiter, if the Minister of Justice is unavailable, Deputy Ministers of Justice, or any other Minister may act as Arbiter in his stead. The Arbiter's role is to maintain order throughout the trial, as well as performing sentencing should the defendant be declared guilty. Any noble of the Conclave may act as the Prosecutor in a Criminal Trial, in the case of multiple nobles offering to act as Prosecutor, the Arbiter of the Trial has the final say in who shall serve as Prosecutor. The Defendant may either represent themselves, or any noble may act as their representative, if the defendant wishes for them to. A jury may be composed of 3 or more nobles of the Conclave of Aldemeria. If three or more nobles are not able and willing to be members of the jury, then the trial proceeds without one. Members of the Jury, and the Arbiter of the Trial may not be called as witnesses.
Criminal Trials follow the following procedure:
  • The Arbiter calls the Trial to order, and identifies the prosecution, defense and jury The Prosecution and Defense swear to remain truthful throughout the trial.
  • Arbiter asks the Prosecution to list all charges laid against the Defendant.
  • Arbiter asks the Defense to enter pleas for each charge.
  • If the Defense pleads guilty to all charges, the trial moves on to sentencing.
  • If the defense pleads not guilty to any charges, the trial continues Prosecution presents their evidence, calls witnesses (who are sworn in upon taking the stand) and makes arguments for the Defendant's guilt.
  • After each witness is done being questioned by the Prosecution, the Arbiter will ask the defense if they wish to Cross-Examine the witness.
  • Defense presents their evidence, calls witnesses (who are sworn in upon taking the stand) and makes arguments for the Defendant's lack of guilt.
  • After each witness is done being questioned by the Defense, the Arbiter will ask the defense if they wish to Cross- Examine the witness.
  • Prosecution makes final statements Defense makes final statements Jury adjourns to deliberate guilt of Defendant on each charge, if they are unable to reach a unanimous consensus, they may request the Arbiter decide guilt.
  • Verdict is delivered by either the Jury or the Arbiter The Arbiter declares sentence for the charges the Defense is found guilty of or plead guilty to.
c. In order for a criminal trial to take place, the evidence must be brought before any Minister, or a Deputy Minister of Justice. If they deem the evidence is sufficient to warrant a trial, it is their responsibility to officially call the trial, and set the date.
d. A Civil Trial is overseen by a Mediator, the preferred Mediator is the Minister of Justice, but if the Minister of Justice is unable to act as Mediator, any other Minister or a Deputy Minister of Justice may act as Mediator. A Civil Trial can be requested for any type of grievance, so long as it is not explicitly the breaking of International law, which would be considered a Criminal Trial, and should be handled as such. Due to the varied nature of these cases, there is no set process to be followed, it is up to the Mediator to decide how the Trial will proceed, but the Mediator must ensure a fair and equal trial for all parties involved. After the Trial, the Mediator pronounces final judgement on the proceedings.
e. In any case where the Minister of Justice is on trial or cannot remain objective in his decisions due to any reason, any other Minister, or deputy Justice minister shall act as Arbiter or Mediator, depending on the type of trial
f. Upon notification to the Ministry of Justice of either a trial or charges being presented against an individual, all evidence shall be presented to the Ministry of Justice upon its discovery. Upon delivery of evidence, the Conclave of Aldemeria becomes the steward of the items, both physical and otherwise, presented as evidence in a judicial trial. The Conclave retains the rights to the evidence in the case of a guilty verdict. In the case of a not guilty verdict, the evidence is returned to the defendant upon completion of the trial.

X. Recognition of Land Claims

a. While the Conclave shall make no attempt to control or limit the lands and waters claimed and developed by its members, it shall formally recognize and defend territory as under the control of a realm only once that land has been subject to a formal claims review under the authority of the Conclave Assembly. Only territory that is formally recognized as under the control of a realm shall benefit from legal recognition and the protections this provides.
b. To be considered a valid land claim and in order to achieve formal recognition as a territory under the control of a realm, the claim must be posted in the Hall of Nations by a noble member of the claiming realm.
If the claimant is not the highest legal authority of that realm, then the highest legal authority must vote in the affirmative during the voting period for the claim to pass.
  • Detailed cartographical documentation of the realm and the proposed expansion
  • Cartographical representation of the territory in relation to the realm and neighboring realms
  • Mandate for expansion with evidence that current lands are being developed to such an extent as to justify expansion
  • Proposal of intended uses for future development within the claimed territory
Following the posting of the claim, a two week period will commence during which time any voting member of the conclave can call for a vote against the land claim. If this vote reaches a greater than 50% majority of votes, the land claim will be considered invalid and the land will remain unclaimed. If no nobles propose a vote against the proposed claim, the request for the recognition of the land claim shall stand.
c. Recognizing that it is the best interests of the Conclave and its citizens that the realms of The Nether and The End continue to be enjoyed by all as international territory for the purposes of infrastructure, research, and exploration, the Conclave shall recognize no claims in the entirety of these realms. These international territories may be freely developed by any party, with all development recognised as being the common heritage of all citizens of the Conclave.
d. The capabilities of the Noble making the land claim, as well as the details of their intentions of their request for Recognition of a Land Claim shall be considered when requests that deviate from the standard claims are made.
e. Definitions and Restrictions:
  • A County is defined as approximately the size of the average large biome. This definition only applies to land claims.
  • A Duchy is defined as approximately the size of three average large biomes. This definition only applies to land claims.
  • Only Noble members of the conclave (rank of Lord or higher) may apply for a land claim.
  • Freeholds and Counties may only request the recognition of a single County per request.
  • Duchies, Principalities, Kingdoms, and Empires may only request the recognition of a single Duchy per request.
  • Only Noble members of the rank Duke and above may make claims for or of NPC realms.
f. Minor realms (also known as NPC Realms) may join the Conclave of Aldemeria as non-voting members. These minor realms may join as either a realm sponsored by multiple realms (a co-build realm) or as a state controlled in some form by a realm of Duchy status or higher:
  • A co-build realm may be started by any noble of the rank of Count or higher. These realms are created with the intention that any members of the Conclave may contribute, under the guidance of the noble or nobles leading the co-build. These realms may not be annexed.
  • An NPC realm may be started by any noble of the rank of Duchy or higher. These realms are created with the intention that the founding noble is free to run the realm as they see fit. These realms may be annexed to the main realm of the noble who operates this realm.
These realms must follow all other rules that regulate the claiming and expanding of land. NPC realms may show up on the official Conclave maps. NPC realms may not count directly towards rank ascensions, with the exception of the ascension to Emperor, where lore must be provided that justifies the NPC realm's inclusion in the 'Empire.'

XI. The Conclave Rules of War

a. Legal War under the Conclave of Aldemeria The Conclave of Aldemeria is a league of realms that have agreed to open trade and a universal peace treaty. However, the Conclave recognizes from time to time that wars may be used to settle disputes. Wars are restricted to combat between realms at war and military infrastructure belonging to those realms. Combat and/or destruction that spreads to individuals and realms not involved in the war may be viewed by the Conclave of Aldemeria as violations against the Conclave itself, and as such are treated as international war crimes and may be prosecuted.
b. Declaring War The highest authority of a realm may declare war legally for any reason by openly declaring war in the Court of Arbitration. A full 24 hours must pass prior to the commencement of hostilities, unless both parties publicly agree to an earlier time. Either realm involved in a legal war may elicit support, however, supporting realms must declare their intention to join the war in the post notifying the Conclave of the Declaration of War. Only the highest authority in a realm may declare the support for their entire realm. Lesser nobles may declare their support.
c. Death and/or Surrender Individuals at war who decease during the war from any cause, either natural or in combat, are considered to have perished in lore and may not engage in further hostilities. Disputed deaths may be confirmed through an inspection of server logs. Individuals at war who wish to surrender may do so through a public notice of surrender to the Court of Arbitration.
d. Conclusion of a Legal War A legal war is considered to have come to its conclusion when one of the following occurs:
  • The highest authority of either the attacking or defending realm deceases.
  • The highest authority of either the attacking or defending realm surrenders publicly through the Court of Arbitration.
  • The highest authority of either the attacking or defending realm has not appeared in game or engaged in war for 3 days. The individual whose activity falls under this situation will be considered to have lost the war. External circumstances may nullify this if notified in the Court of Arbitration.
  • The highest authority of both the attacking and defending realms sign a formal declaration of peace in the Court of Arbitration.
e. War Demands A legal war typically will have some kind of demand. The following demands are considered legal:
  • Prevention of land claims and annexation of land. Annexation of land may only occur when the two realms are close neighbors.
  • Demanding payment for damaged goods, property, or honor. Failing to follow through with a trade agreement falls under this.
  • Other demands may be considered legal by the standing Justice Minister if requested in the Court of Arbitration
f. Materials of Great Destruction Buckets of Lava, TNT, and Fire as generated by a Fire Charge or Flint and Steel are legally recognized as Materials of Great Destruction, items which when used are capable of causing great amounts of destruction over a large area. These materials may be used in legal war during combat so long as the use of these is constrained to the immediate area of combat. The offensive and/or destructive use of these materials in areas outside of legal military conflict may be recognized as a war crime. The destruction of settlements, defensive structures, and other areas is not an acceptable use of these materials during a legally declared war.
g. War Crimes Individuals or realms found to have committed actions during the duration of a legally declared war that violate either the Conclave Rules of War or the Conclave Criminal Code may find their actions investigated by a War Crimes Tribunal. A War Crimes Tribunal may take place after the formal cessation of hostilities at the discretion of the Justice Minister.
h. Lore War/Battle A 'Lore War' is a type of legal war or battle that may be declared between individuals or realms who have previously agreed on a time and place to engage in battle for mutual enjoyment. Prior to the commencement of hostilities, all parties may agree to terms to be adhered to during combat; as contained events, Lore Wars shall not be governed by the terms of the Conclave Rules of War. Individuals found to have committed actions during the duration of a lore war that violate the agreed-upon terms may be held liable through a civil trial if necessary.

XII. Inactivity

a. Any voting member of the Conclave of Aldemeria can declare themselves voluntarily inactive at any time in an official post, from the time they declare themselves such their votes will not be considered for any voting majority thresholds. At anytime after declaring themselves inactive, they may regain activity be simply declaring themselves active again in an official post, from which point their votes shall once again be counted towards voting majority thresholds.
b. Any voting member of the Conclave of Aldemeria who has not actively participated on the forums or on the server for a period of two weeks, shall be declared involuntarily inactive, from which point their vote shall not be counted towards voting threshold majorities. To regain activity, the member must simply re-state their activity in an official post on the forums
c. Any voting member of the Conclave who is not seen within the lands of Aldemeria (server presence) and does not participate in Conclave governance (legal discussion/voting on the forum) for a period of six months or more shall be considered a 'Non-Participating Member of the Conclave of Aldemeria.' From this time, they lose their votes within the Conclave and will not be considered for voting thresholds. Non-Participating Members of the Conclave of Aldemeria will still be recognized as part of the Conclave, as will the votes of any members of that realm who remain active.
d. Any Non-Participating Member of the Conclave of Aldemeria who is not seen within the lands of Aldemeria (server presence) and does not participate in Conclave governance (legal discussion/voting on the forum) for an additional period of six months or more shall be considered a 'Withdrawn Member of the Conclave of Aldemeria' and have abdicated their throne. A Non-Participating Member of the Conclave of Aldemeria is considered to still be inactive regardless of any activity if they do not successfully restore their right to vote first.
e. Having abdicated their throne, their realm is open to claims from vassals still living within the realm: the highest ranking vassal of the realm from this time shall serve as the liege/ruler of the realm. Should there be two vassals of the same rank, the realm may be split between them. The new ruling house(s) may not include the builds of the former ruling house for their own rank progression. Additionally, should no vassals claim the realm for their own, foreign realms that directly border (via land) the abdicated realm may claim the realm as a 'vassal state.' This 'vassal state' cannot be integrated with the new suzerain realm. New rulers may not build in the finished counties of the former realm, with the exception of improving infrastructure. Improvements made to finished areas of the vassal realm may not be considered for rank advancement. Empty areas of the vassalized realm may be annexed directly to the suzerain realm.
f. To restore their status as a full member of the Conclave, the Non-Participating or Withdrawn Member must create a post declaring their return to activity affirming their dedication to the Conclave, once they feel they have proven their intention to return to activity. A vote of the Conclave requiring a clear majority (greater than 50% of the active votes of the Conclave) must be passed to show the community believes that the abdicated member intends to become active again. Non-Participating Members are returned their privilege to vote. Withdrawn Members are returned to their position as the ruler of the lands containing their former builds, as well as their right to vote.
g. Should a Withdrawn Member successfully earn the right to vote and the right to rule their lands again, they will not be able to recover areas outside of the counties where they personally built, i.e. areas of their realm they did not build in, areas vassals constructed, and areas that were claimed by not built in may not be kept by default. Should disagreements result, and an agreement cannot be made between the involved parties, the land dispute may proceed to a trial or to a war.

XIII. Amending of this Charter

a. Unless otherwise specified, any and all elements of this Charter may be amended or otherwise altered only with the support of a greater than 66% majority of votes cast in a motion brought before the Conclave Assembly.
b. This specific section concerning the percentage needed to amend the Charter may only be altered with a 100% unanimous vote of the Conclave
c. The Conclave may never amend the Charter to remove the basic rights granted to all members.
